Healthy and hearty mexican pork chops cooked with salsa

Lets get to cooking some delicious mexican food. This is a staple in many mexican households. With fresh delicious tomatoes, tomatillos, serrano peppers you can make a dish that your family will beg for!

Ingredients

  • 3 tomatoes
  • 3 tomatillos
  • 1/4 of white onion
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • cilantro
  • 2 pound of pork chops
  • 1.5 tablespoon of chicken bouillon
  • 1 tablespoon of salt

Steps

  1. Wash the produce with baking soda for at least 15 minutes and start your oven, set to broil for 400 degrees F
  2. While the produce gets cleaned, make your rice and beans
  3. Once the produce is cleaned, dry it, place the tomatoes, tomatillos and serrano peppers on a baking sheet. Bake until the top is charred and then flip - about 10 minutes one each side. Once done cool them a plate.
  4. While the veggies cool, start the porkchops. Season lightly with salt, garlic powder, onion powder and paprika
  5. Using medium-high heat, heat up some oil and place the pork chops in. Cook for about 3 minutes on each side. They will finish cooking in the salsa. When you flip the pork chop over, place some chopped onions into the pan. Remove from pan and set aside.
  6. Put the cooled veggies into a blender with the onion, garlic, chicken boullion, salt and fresh cilantro. Blend until smooth. If too chunky go ahead and add some water.
  7. Pour the salsa into the pan with the pork chop juices and simmer on medium-low. Once simmering, place porkchops back into the pan and simmer for about 15 minutes.
