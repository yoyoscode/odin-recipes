Mexican green spaghetti

Another staple in mexican households and my childhood favorite meal. This can be made with chicken on the side!

Ingredients

  • 4 poblano peppers
  • 1/4 of white onion
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • cilantro
  • hondurean or salvadorean sour cream
  • 1.5 tablespoon of chicken bouillon
  • 1 tablespoon of salt

Steps

  1. Wash the produce with baking soda for at least 15 minutes and start your oven, set to broil for 400 degrees F
  2. While the produce gets cleaned, start your pasta water
  3. Once the produce is cleaned, dry it, poblano peppers on a baking sheet. Bake until the top is charred and then flip - about 10 minutes one each side. Once done cool them in a bowl with a lid.
  4. While the veggies cool, start the chicken. Season lightly with salt, garlic powder, onion powder and paprika and cook throughly.
  5. Scrape the charred layered from the poblano peppers and place them in a blender with the onion, garlic, sour cream, chicken boullion, salt and fresh cilantro. Blend until smooth. If too chunky go ahead and add some water.
  6. Pour the salsa into a new pan or you can use the chicken pan, simmer on medium-low.
